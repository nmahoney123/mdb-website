Since 1985, Mahoney Design & Build has grown the way we prefer to grow: through owners who came back and owners they referred. That repeat-and-referral base, which accounts for the large majority of our work, was built in the Northeast from our headquarters in Oneida, New York. As our clients have expanded their own portfolios, we have expanded with them — establishing offices in Chicago, Illinois, and Bend, Oregon, so the firm now serves the Northeast, Midwest, and Pacific Northwest.

Opening in new regions raises a fair question from any owner: does a firm's standard travel? A reputation earned over decades in one market is only worth something in another if the thing that earned it comes along. This is how we think about carrying our standard across regions without diluting it.

## Why we expanded toward our clients, not away from them

Geographic growth can be driven by two very different impulses. One is chasing new markets for their own sake — planting a flag where the work looks busy and hoping to win a foothold. The other is following the clients you already serve as their own footprints grow. Our expansion is the second kind. Self-storage, hospitality, and multifamily developers rarely stay in one metro, and the owners who trust a builder in one region are the natural first clients for that builder in the next.

That distinction matters because it sets the terms of the relationship. Entering a market alongside a client you have already delivered for means the standard is already known on both sides. The expansion is an extension of an existing relationship rather than a cold start, which is the most reliable way we know to keep quality intact as geography widens.

## What stays the same in every office

The core of what MDB delivers is not tied to a location. Single-contract design-build, where one accountable team owns both the drawing and the number, works the same in Bend as it does in Oneida. So does the discipline underneath it: preconstruction estimating that keeps the design tethered to the budget, cost certainty backed by a firm number before an owner commits to build, and a schedule built to hold rather than to impress.

Those are methods, not local customs, and they travel intact. An owner working with our Chicago or Bend office should expect the same preconstruction rigor, the same single point of accountability, and the same measured approach to cost and schedule that our Northeast clients have relied on for decades. The market changes; the model does not.

## What has to be local

Consistency does not mean pretending every region is the same. It emphatically is not, and a firm that ignores that does not deliver a consistent product — it delivers a portable set of mistakes. Real regional competence has to be built market by market.

Building codes and their local amendments differ, and so do the authorities who enforce them and the pace at which they do. Climate drives real design divergence — the snow and freeze-thaw exposure of the Northeast, the seismic and wildfire considerations of the Pacific Northwest, the wind and temperature swings of the Midwest each shape how a building has to be detailed. Subcontractor markets, material lead times, and labor availability vary by region and by cycle. And entitlement and permitting timelines run on each municipality's calendar, not a national one.

Delivering consistent quality means holding the method constant while adapting the execution to these local realities — pairing our approach with genuine knowledge of the codes, trades, and approval processes in each region we serve.

## How consistency is actually protected

The bridge between a constant method and a local execution is the way work is planned and run. Our preconstruction process is the same everywhere, which means the questions we ask about a site — its zoning, its soils, its stormwater, its long-lead risks — are the same questions in every market, even when the answers are entirely local. A disciplined, repeatable process is what lets a firm operate in three regions and still recognize its own work in each of them.

Just as important is the people standard. Projects are run by superintendents and project managers, and the experience an owner has is largely the experience those people deliver. Extending into a new region is, more than anything, a commitment to bringing the same field standard and the same accountability to the teams who run the jobs there. A consistent method executed by teams held to a consistent standard is what keeps quality from thinning out as the map widens.

## What this means for owners

For an owner in the Midwest or the Pacific Northwest, the practical effect is access to a design-build firm with a long Northeast track record in self-storage, hospitality, and multifamily — now with local offices in Chicago and Bend to pair that experience with regional knowledge. Our sister brand, Self Storage Builders, brings the same depth to storage development, with over a million square feet built. The intent of the expansion is simple: to make the standard our repeat clients already know available in more of the places they build.

## The bottom line

Expanding from Oneida into Chicago and Bend was about following our clients, not chasing markets. The method that earned our repeat-and-referral base — single-contract design-build, preconstruction discipline, cost certainty, and schedule reliability — travels intact, while codes, climate, and local trades are respected as the genuinely regional matters they are. Consistent quality across regions is not a slogan; it is a constant process executed by teams held to a constant standard, wherever the work is.
